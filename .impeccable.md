# Design Context

## Target Audience
Compliance officers, legal teams, AI engineers, and startup founders who need to understand and comply with the EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689). They are busy professionals, often non-technical, who need clarity not complexity.

## Use Cases
- Classify an AI system's risk tier under the Act
- Track compliance obligations with interactive checklists
- Generate required documentation from templates
- Monitor enforcement deadlines
- Share classification results with colleagues

## Brand Personality / Tone
Institutional credibility meets modern tooling. Think gov.uk's clarity with Linear's polish. Serious subject matter treated with respect, not fear-mongering. Trustworthy, precise, calm authority. The EU flag blue (#003399) and deep navy (#1a1f36) anchor the palette. No playfulness - this is regulation, not entertainment.

## Typography
DM Serif Display for headings (institutional weight), DM Sans for body (clean readability). Monospace (JetBrains Mono) for article references and code.

## Color Palette
- Navy (#1a1f36) - primary backgrounds, text
- EU Blue (#003399) - actions, links, accents
- Risk tiers: Red (prohibited), Orange (high-risk), Amber (GPAI), Blue (limited), Green (minimal)
- Tinted slate neutrals throughout (never pure gray)

## Constraints
- Next.js 14, Tailwind CSS, no component library (Radix planned)
- Must work fully client-side (no backend)
- WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility
- i18n: 18 languages planned
- Mobile-first responsive
