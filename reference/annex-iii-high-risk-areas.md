# Annex III: High-Risk AI System Areas

The following areas are designated as high-risk under the EU AI Act. AI systems used in these areas must comply with Articles 9-15.

---

## 1. Biometrics

AI systems intended for:
- Remote biometric identification (not real-time in public spaces for law enforcement, which is prohibited)
- Biometric categorization based on sensitive or protected attributes
- Emotion recognition

## 2. Critical Infrastructure

AI systems intended as safety components in the management and operation of:
- Road traffic
- Water supply
- Gas supply
- Heating supply
- Electricity supply

## 3. Education and Vocational Training

AI systems intended for:
- Determining access to or admission into educational or vocational training institutions
- Evaluating learning outcomes, including to steer the learning process
- Determining the appropriate level of education for an individual
- Monitoring and detecting prohibited behavior of students during tests

## 4. Employment, Workers Management, Self-Employment

AI systems intended for:
- Recruitment or selection, particularly for placing targeted job advertisements, filtering applications, evaluating candidates
- Making decisions affecting terms of work-related relationships, promotion, or termination
- Allocating tasks based on individual behavior, personal traits, or characteristics
- Monitoring and evaluating performance and behavior of persons in such relationships

## 5. Access to Essential Private and Public Services

AI systems intended for:
- Evaluating eligibility for public assistance benefits and services (or revoking/reducing them)
- Evaluating creditworthiness of natural persons (except fraud detection)
- Risk assessment and pricing in life and health insurance
- Evaluating and classifying emergency calls, including for dispatching priority

## 6. Law Enforcement

AI systems intended for:
- Individual risk assessment (likelihood of offending/re-offending, or of being a victim)
- As polygraphs or similar tools to detect deception
- Evaluating the reliability of evidence in investigation or prosecution
- Profiling of natural persons in detection, investigation, or prosecution of criminal offences

## 7. Migration, Asylum, and Border Control

AI systems intended for:
- Assessing risk of irregular migration or health risk
- Examining applications for asylum, visa, or residence permits and associated complaints
- Detecting, recognizing, or identifying natural persons (except verification of travel documents)

## 8. Administration of Justice and Democratic Processes

AI systems intended for:
- Assisting judicial authorities in researching and interpreting facts and the law, and applying it to concrete cases
- Influencing the outcome of an election or referendum (excluding organizational tools not directly influencing votes)
